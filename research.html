My Research Page

I focus on the study of galaxy clusters and the large scale structure. I am mainly involved in three projects: the Atacama Cosmology Telescope (ACT), the Canadian Cluster Comparison Project (CCCP), the Kilo-Degree Survey (KiDS). Below is a short description of them and other projects in which I participate.

Surveys

Atacama Cosmology Telescope (ACT)
ACT is a 6-meter, millimeter-band telescope located in northern Chile. Its main objectives are to study the CMB in small scales (~1 arcmin) and clusters of galaxies through the Sunyaev-Zel'dovich Effect. The picture on the right shows the spectacular El Gordo, a merging galaxy cluster at z=0.87 discovered by ACT.
Canadian Cluster Comparison Project (CCCP)
The CCCP is a multi-wavelength study of about 50 massive galaxy clusters, mainly through weak gravitational lensing and X-ray observations. The major aim is to understand what impact do different astrophysical processes have on clusters and how is this reflected in different scaling relations. Shown in the right is Abell 1914, one of the clusters in the sample.
Kilo-Degree Survey (KiDS)
KiDS is one of the public surveys that is being observed with the VLT Survey Telescope. When completed, it will have observed 1,500 square degrees in two areas in the sky in the u, g, r, i bands with high image quality. The main science driver of the survey is the study of cosmic shear, meaning gravitational lensing by large scale structure. To the right, a picture of the 2.2m VST which is located in Chile.
